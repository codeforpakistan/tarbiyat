Position Information

Basic Details

Position Title

  • Use clear, descriptive titles
  • Include department/division if relevant
  • Examples:
    • "Software Development Intern - IT Department"
    • "Policy Research Intern - Economic Affairs"
    • "Digital Marketing Intern - Public Relations"

Department/Division

  • Specify the exact department
  • Help students understand reporting structure
  • Provides context for the role

Position Description

Write Compelling Descriptions

Include the following sections:

Role Overview (2-3 sentences)

This internship provides hands-on experience in government policy research and analysis. The intern will work directly with our policy team to support evidence-based decision making processes.

Key Responsibilities (4-6 bullet points)
  • Research and analyze policy documents
  • Assist in preparing briefing materials
  • Support stakeholder consultation processes
  • Contribute to policy recommendations
  • Maintain databases and documentation
Learning Objectives (3-4 bullet points)
  • Understand government policy development cycle
  • Develop analytical and research skills
  • Gain exposure to stakeholder engagement
  • Learn project management techniques
Day-to-Day Activities

Provide specific examples of what interns will do:

  • Morning briefings with the policy team
  • Research sessions using government databases
  • Draft preparation and review meetings
  • Stakeholder interviews and note-taking
  • Weekly progress reviews with supervisor

Position Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • Field of Study: Select relevant academic fields
  • Academic Level: Specify minimum degree level
  • Year of Study: Current year or semester requirements
  • GPA Requirements: Minimum academic performance (if applicable)

Skills and Competencies

Technical Skills

  • Software proficiency (MS Office, specific tools)
  • Programming languages (if relevant)
  • Research methodologies
  • Data analysis capabilities

Soft Skills

  • Communication skills (written and verbal)
  • Analytical thinking
  • Time management
  • Teamwork and collaboration

Practical Details

Duration and Schedule

  • Duration: Specify internship length (e.g., 12 weeks)
  • Start Date: Preferred or flexible start dates
  • Working Hours: Daily schedule and weekly hours
  • Flexibility: Remote work options, if available

Compensation and Benefits

  • Stipend: Monthly allowance amount (if provided)
  • Transportation: Travel allowance or arrangements
  • Learning Budget: Training or conference opportunities
  • Certification: Completion certificate details

Important: Be transparent about compensation. If the position is unpaid, highlight other valuable benefits like learning opportunities, certificates, and networking.

Application Process

Required Documents

  • Current resume/CV
  • Cover letter addressing position requirements
  • Academic transcripts
  • Letters of recommendation (if required)
  • Portfolio samples (for relevant positions)

Selection Criteria

  1. Academic Merit: GPA and relevant coursework
  2. Relevant Experience: Previous internships or projects
  3. Skill Match: Alignment with position requirements
  4. Motivation: Demonstrated interest in the field
  5. Availability: Commitment to full duration

Best Practices

‚úÖ Do's

  • Provide detailed, realistic job descriptions
  • Include specific learning outcomes
  • Be clear about expectations and deliverables
  • Offer meaningful work, not just busy work
  • Specify mentor support and guidance available
  • Include growth and networking opportunities

‚ùå Don'ts

  • Don't use vague or misleading position titles
  • Don't set unrealistic requirements for entry-level positions
  • Don't promise learning opportunities you can't deliver
  • Don't ignore applications or delay responses
  • Don't create positions just for administrative tasks
  • Don't forget to update position status regularly

After Publishing

Managing Applications

  • Review applications promptly
  • Provide feedback to all applicants
  • Schedule interviews efficiently
  • Communicate decisions clearly
  • Update position status when filled

Position Analytics

Monitor your position performance:

  • View Count: How many students viewed your position
  • Application Rate: Percentage of viewers who applied
  • Applicant Quality: Average qualifications of applicants
  • Time to Fill: How long it took to fill the position

Pro Tip: Great internship positions lead to great interns. Invest time in creating detailed, attractive position descriptions to attract the best candidates.
